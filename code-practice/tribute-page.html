The String Cheese Incident

The God Fathers of the famous music festival, Electric Forest.

The String Cheese Incident is a jam band that founded the Musis festival, Electric Forest. Beforehand, known as Rothbury Music Festival, Electric Forest is an music festival that has a vast variety of musicians of all genres come and play music for festival goers. The festival goers, known as Forest Family enjoy all generes of music, and especially Electronic music. So they can forgot everything about their real lives, head bang, and party to their desires.

Here’s a timeline of their History

  • 1996-2001 – The band provided a scenario to the origin of the name on October 6, 1996, at the Mishawaka Amphitheater in Bellvue, Colorado, citing an incident during which Kang broke a mandolin string.

  • 2002-2004 – In October 2003, SCI released their fourth studio album, Untying the Not, produced by Martin “Youth” Glover, formerly of the band the Killing Joke. The release continued SCI’s use of electronic music and trance elements.

  • 2005-2006 – In June 2005, the band released their fifth studio album, titled One Step Closer, containing thirteen original tracks with guest songwriting collaborations. In 2005, the band also welcomed percussionist Jason Hann.

  • 2007 – The band announced a few shows for 2007, including their annual Winter Carnival (sans 2006), which stopped in Denver and Vail Colorado, and an appearance at the Bonnaroo Music Festival as well as the 10,000 Lakes Festival.

  • 2008 – All members were present at the Rothbury Music Festival in Rothbury, Michigan, performing with their respective solo projects.

  • 2009 – On March 16, 2009, it was announced that the String Cheese Incident would reunite for the second annual Rothbury Festival. This was to be their only show of 2009.

  • 2010 – On February 2nd, 2010, it was announced that String Cheese Incident would reunite for seven shows in the summer

  • 2011 – In Summer 2011, String Cheese played the Which Stage at Bonnaroo Music Festival in Manchester, Tennessee on June 11th.[13] Later that summer, String Cheese was featured as a headliner for three out of four nights (July 1st–3rd) of the opening year for the Electric Forest Festival,[14] an event which the band hosted in Rothbury, Michigan.

  • 2014 – In 2014 the String Cheese Incident released “A Song In My Head”, their first studio album since 2005.

  • 2015 – In June 2015, the String Cheese Incident invited Skrillex, a well-known American electronic music artist, to jam with them at the Electric Forest festival.

  • 2016-2017 – The String Cheese Incident continued to tour, and released the “Believe” recording in 2017.
